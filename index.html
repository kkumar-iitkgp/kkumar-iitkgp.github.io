About Me

Hello! I'm Kuldeep Kumar!!!
While rare and common variants both contribute to the risk of psychiatric conditions and influence brain structure, these research areas often operate in silos. My research cross-examines the risk associated with rare and common variants for psychiatric conditions and explores how these variants impact brain structure as measured by Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) in humans. To achieve this, I leverage recent advances in functional genomics to connect psychiatric and neuroimaging genetics, aiming to understand how large-scale brain networks may underlie the link between genomic variants and psychiatric disorders.
Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher in Professor Sebastien Jacquemont’s lab at the CHUSJ Research Center, University of Montréal, where I study the effects of rare variants, like CNVs and SNVs, on cognition and brain structure.
I earned my Ph.D. in Applied Research from ÉTS in Montreal, focusing on multi-modal MRI analysis using machine learning to develop brain fingerprints. I briefly worked as a Scientist at the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), including the Mars Orbiter Mission.

Research Interests:
- Neuroimaging genetics
- Genetic variants
- Multi-omics
- Medical image analysis
- Big data and Machine Learning
